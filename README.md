#### Sr. Process Engineer — Fuel Cell Slurry Manufacturing
*April 2022 – June 2025*

This three-year assignment spanned the full GM project cycle: SOR authorship (including the trademark three-month pull-ahead), supplier bidding, design, funding realignment, fabrication, and machine run-off.

Anticipating a major volume step-up from Gen2 to Gen3, I scrapped much of the Gen2 SOR and design philosophy (small, mobile pilot tanks moved between stations) and proposed a traditional interconnected stainless steel tank system.

Highlights:

- **Powder introduction redesign** — Gen2 plant logs identified powder as the lowest-accuracy dispense step. Engineered a two-stage gain/loss hopper with machine learning built into the bottom dispense to account for pressure variation and material variability inherent in introducing material into the inerted environment required for FC ink grinding
- **LOT traceability system** — developed semi-automated raw material dispensing logic that held cross-LOT liquid blending to under 1% and could be operated by a single technician managing container staging and QC
- **Local supplier strategy** — opted for Michigan-based fabrication, which kept our timeline intact while downstream counterparts experienced delays with overseas vendors
- **Non-hazardous surrogate ink** — designed a low-cost surrogate that closely replicated processing properties, dramatically reducing scrap costs and providing abundant material for run-off validation, enabling individual dispense validation to target tolerances before automation evaluation
- **NFPA & Michigan Building Code compliance** — served as primary point of contact at two separate installation sites, managing hazardous material storage and movement requirements; worked with Ghafari on utility supply
- **Moisture contamination alerting system** — developed a new logic to quarantine ink and alert downstream operations if gaseous supply became contaminated with moisture

Led GM Fuel Cell's first connected tank system installation. Project was halted when government grants funding the sheeting and stacking process were withdrawn and customer subsidies were cancelled, ultimately leading to the closure of GM's fuel cell program.

#### Chemist (Intern → Full-time)
*February 2012 – February 2014*

Joined Resinate during my final year of undergrad at the University of Michigan. The company was a well-funded startup producing CASE-industry products (coatings, adhesives, sealants, elastomers) from PET derivatives sourced from recycled water bottles and a range of renewable fatty acids.

Polymer synthesis isn't a significant part of the standard chemical engineering curriculum, but I was quickly drawn in by the complexity of the process. A single data point could require up to 8 hours of PET digestion, followed by 24+ hours of additive branching, esterification, and distillation — plus dispersion processing for water-based products, a careful stripping procedure via rotary evaporation, difficult endpoint titrations, and several days of physical and chemical resistivity QC. All of that to earn one data point, then clean 10,000 cP polymer out of the glassware to start again.

I didn't fully appreciate it at the time, but that experience permanently shaped how I value aggregate data and why I push to understand the full theoretical picture before drawing conclusions.
